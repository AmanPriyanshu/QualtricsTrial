How does our system work?

Silent Labs implements a cryptographically secure privacy layer within open banking systems, fundamentally transforming how financial data access is governed and enforced. Our system integrates with account aggregators to establish a zero-trust privacy framework through advanced Multi-Party Computation (MPC) and Threshold Signature Schemes (TSS).

When you connect your financial accounts through our platform, we deploy a distributed cryptographic protocol that mathematically binds your privacy preferences to the data processing pipeline. Unlike traditional systems that rely on policy-based access controls, our implementation uses threshold cryptography to split your authorization keys across multiple parties. This ensures that data access requires cryptographic consensus that aligns with your specified privacy parameters.

What happens to my data?

The core of our technology leverages MPC to enable third parties to compute authorized inferences without ever accessing the underlying raw financial data. Through our proprietary implementation of secure multiparty computation protocols, we create cryptographic circuits that precisely match your consent specifications.

Each inference request triggers a distributed computation protocol where the raw data remains encrypted and fragmented. Our TSS implementation ensures that signing keys for data access are never reconstructed in a single location, while our MPC protocol enforces that only pre-approved computational circuits can be executed.

What protections do I have?
  • You maintain complete control over what information third parties can access
  • Third parties only receive specific insights you've authorized, never your raw financial data
  • Our proprietary technology mathematically enforces these privacy controls, making unauthorized access impossible
